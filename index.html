Build a Career Not a GET RICH QUICK Approach

Today, I had the chance to apply for an internship at the British High Commission office in Nigeria. It came with an attractive salary and benefits, but I chose to let it pass. Reading through the job requirements, I realized I was overqualified—holding a master’s degree for a role that demanded far less. Yet, what struck me wasn’t the opportunity itself, but the clarity it brought about my goals. I’ve resolved to build a career in software development, one rooted in purpose and long-term impact. As I reflected, the words of Oyedepo echoed in my mind:
“You will not find a story of outstanding success without a story of sacrifice behind it.”
Building a career isn’t about chasing the highest paycheck; it’s about investing in skills, embracing growth, and staying true to a vision that transcends immediate gains. Then, the paychecks will inevitably roll in, as money flows in the direction of value.
